Frosh Week

Frosh Week is a week of fun events that welcomes and connects new undergraduate students to the CS community at SFU. It is run by the CSSS at SFU.

Who is "the CSSS"? (???)

The Computing Science Student Society (CSSS) is a student-led organization at Simon Fraser University that represents undergraduate students studying Computing Science.

Students that are declared Computing Science majors, minors, or are taking CS courses, are automatically members of the society.

To connect with the CSSS, check out our Discord server, or visit our two common rooms located in the Applied Science Building at SFU's Burnaby campus: ASB 9802, and ASB 9971.

What is RETRO FROSH?

RETRO FROSH is the name given to the Frosh Week of 2023.

Every Frosh Week has a theme: for example, last year's was Candy Frosh, the year before that... something to do with Zelda, I think.

This year is SFU's School of Computing Science's 50th anniversary. To acknowledge this, we decided to theme 2023's Frosh Week as RETRO FROSH.

When?

RETRO FROSH starts Monday, September 11th and continues until Saturday, September 16th. Information about the time and location of each event can be found in the schedule.

How can I join?

Please Sign-Up to secure your place in RETRO FROSH. Attendees receive a free RETRO FROSH themed T-Shirt, as well as several free meals throughout the week.

Frequently Asked Questions

Frosh Week FAQ

Where will the events take place?

All events take place at the Simon Fraser University Burnaby campus. The locations per event can be found in the schedule.

If you are having difficulty locating us, do not hesitate to contact Micah or Isabelle. (Contact information can be found in the CSSS Discord Server.)

Do I need to bring any laptops or other electronic devices?

You do not need to bring anything except for yourself.

During PacMacro, however, access to a mobile device with an internet connection is necessary.

What is PacMacro?

PacMacro is essentially Pacman in real life. Utilizing CSSS-developed software, the game will keep track of players' positions on campus, and display the locations of other players.

I can't come to every event, or am not free for the entire duration of an event. Is this OK?

You can come and go as you please during Frosh Week. Late arrivals or early departures are totally OK. If you cannot attend some events due to other commitments, that is fine also.

SFU FAQ

Why is it called "Computing Science", not "Computer Science"?

CS at SFU has a long history. Way back in '73 when the School of Computing Science started at SFU, either "Computer Science" wasn't an idiom for the major yet, or we just decided to be different.

Recommended Courses?

Please contact FAS advising for course information and degree planning.

I cannot find a room!

SFU provides a very handy tool called the Room Finder. Simply input the room number (e.g., ASB 9703), and it will highlight the room on an interactive campus map. This works for the Burnaby, Surrey, and Vancouver campuses.
